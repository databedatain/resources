DOMESTIC VIOLENCE

What is Domestic Violence?

Domestic violence is a pattern of behaviors used by one partner to maintain power and control over another in an intimate relationship. It can include physical, emotional, sexual, financial, and psychological abuse. It affects people of all ages, genders, and backgrounds.

What Kind of Resources Are There?

There are hotlines for immediate support, shelters for safe housing, legal advocacy for orders of protection, counseling services, support groups, and help with safety planning. Many services are free and confidential.

What Happens When I Call a Hotline?

A trained advocate will listen without judgment. They can help you create a safety plan, connect you with local resources, and support whatever decisions you make. You don't have to give your name.

How do I get help to get started?

Call any of the hotlines listed here, or reach out to CHBS directly. Staff can assist with referrals, safety planning, and connecting you with local services. Walk-ins are welcome at any of our offices.
